## Density recommendation

This chapter is structural and instrument-centric rather than numeric. Its load-bearing concepts are: the brand-formation loop (a process chain with a single "marketing acts here" node), the explicit-vs-implicit instrument contrast, the conjoint/DCE part-worth isolation mechanism, the WEAT setup, the decay-curve durability test, and the integrated three-instrument study design. The author has stubbed five of these. The PQ heuristic is mostly weak here — the IAT validity numbers (r ≈ .274 vs r ≈ .148, CIs spanning zero) are the one genuine effect-size story, and the ICER 81% figure is a single magnitude — so the chapter is carried by VG (structural/before-after claims) and MC (process) figures, with one forest/dot chart for the contested IAT validity.

Recommended density: **6 figures**. Medium-high density. FIREWALL note: the brand-equity-vs-clinical-value correlation is an *untested open question* and must never be drawn as established — any figure touching it shows it as a hypothesis/open node, not a measured relationship. The IAT and brand-WEAT applications are explicitly contested/unvalidated; the validity figure must show the dispute (two effect sizes, CIs crossing zero), and the WEAT/IAT brand-application gap must read as "proposal built from validated parts," not as an existing result.

## Video candidate pass

**FIGURE 5 — Durability decay curve (durable vs transient)**
- **Status:** Recommend as video candidate (single, ~1/chapter).
- **Criterion:** Transition mechanism is the learning target + the essential point is temporal. The chapter's methodological lesson is explicitly "about time": that two outcomes look identical at the immediate post-test and only reveal themselves at the delayed re-measurement. That is a time-evolution where the *decay itself* — one line holding, the other falling away — is the content. A static chart shows the endpoint; the danger the chapter warns against (booking the immediate spike as equity) is precisely the frame before divergence.
- **Reason:** Animating the two lines rising together to an identical immediate-post peak, then holding the frame at that peak (where "brand teams routinely book that spike as equity"), then advancing time so the transient line decays back to baseline while the durable line holds — dramatizes why one post-test cannot distinguish the patterns. The viewer experiences the false equivalence and then its resolution, which is the chapter's central pedagogical move.
- **Suggested format:** 12–16s silent loop, white background, Okabe-Ito; phase 1 both lines rise to identical immediate-post peak, phase 2 brief hold at the peak (the deceptive moment), phase 3 time advances to delayed post — transient line falls toward baseline, durable line stays elevated, divergence resolving. No narration text. Recommend; do not auto-select.
