# AI-Driven Programmatic HCP Marketing — Vision

**Author:** Nik Bear Brown

*Tik TOC Phase 1: Vision and Positioning.*

---

## Book Concept Summary

This book teaches pharma marketing Fellows, analysts, and innovation teams how to translate emerging AI research into testable product opportunities for HCP marketing and brand measurement. It fills the gap between pharma brand planning, vendor claims about AI-driven lift, and the academic research frontier in models such as ensembles, Mixture of Experts, uplift modeling, causal inference, and LLM-based measurement.

The book succeeds if a reader can produce a credible research-to-product brief: a clearly stated commercial hypothesis, a model or measurement idea from current research, a proposed experiment, the brand or marketing KPI it affects, the evidence needed to justify investment, and the compliance and patient-welfare risks that must be handled before productization.

## Book Type and Deployment Specification

**Book type:** Practitioner Handbook with course-textbook structure.

**Primary adoption context:** Fellowship programs, commercial innovation labs, pharma analytics training, HCP marketing platform onboarding, and graduate/professional courses in health marketing analytics.

**Secondary adoption context:** Product strategy teams at pharma marketing platforms, agency innovation teams, brand analytics teams, and health AI seminars.

**What the book is explicitly not designed for:** It is not a general introduction to pharmaceutical marketing, a production ML engineering manual, a regulatory law textbook, or a vendor sales deck.

**How the TOC signals book type:** Each chapter builds a practical capability and culminates in a Fellow-style research artifact: map, metric definition, model comparison, experiment design, brand association study, or product handoff memo.

## Learner Profile

**Primary reader:** A Fellow working with or near a pharma marketing company who is asked to find external research ideas that might improve product performance.

**Prior knowledge safe to assume:**

- Basic pharma commercial vocabulary: brand, indication, HCP, NPI, script, claims, payer, MLR.
- Basic analytics literacy: prediction, experiment, metric, bias, model, validation.
- Comfort reading short research summaries, vendor claims, and product documentation.

**Prior misconceptions:**

- That script lift and brand equity are the same thing.
- That the newest model family is automatically better than gradient boosting on tabular data.
- That "Mixture of Experts" in vendor language always means a true end-to-end sparse neural architecture.
- That engagement metrics prove clinical or brand impact.
- That compliance review begins after productization rather than during research design.

**Current capability gap:** The reader can understand individual pieces of pharma marketing or AI research, but cannot yet turn a paper, model idea, or vendor claim into a disciplined product-relevant research proposal.

**Motivation type:** Professional and intellectual. The reader wants to make useful discoveries without overclaiming, and wants their research to be taken seriously by product, data science, commercial, and MLR stakeholders.

**Front-loading decision:** The book opens with the commercial system before models. A Fellow who cannot name the KPI, data source, stakeholder, and risk surface cannot evaluate whether a model matters.

## Central Argument

The next useful innovation in pharma marketing AI will not come from copying model names from the LLM world into sales decks. It will come from disciplined translation: matching current research to measurable commercial and brand problems, testing it cheaply, and handing only credible ideas into the product roadmap.

Fellows can be unusually valuable in this system because they sit outside the pressure to ship immediately. They can ask whether a model improves incrementality rather than propensity, whether a content system changes brand association rather than opens, whether a measurement design survives independent scrutiny, and whether the claimed optimization creates patient value or just branded volume.

## Field Positioning

### Comparable Text 1

- **Title / Author / Year:** Modern Healthcare Marketing in the Digital Era / Kakhaber Djakeli / 2024.
- **What it covers that this book also covers:** Digital health marketing, healthcare brand-building, and channel strategy.
- **What it misses that this book addresses:** NPI-level programmatic HCP targeting, point-of-care AI, MoE versus ensemble architecture, uplift modeling, and the Fellow innovation-lab workflow.
- **Why choose this book:** This book is built around research-to-product action, not general channel description.

### Comparable Text 2

- **Title / Author / Year:** Pharmaceutical Marketing / industry and academic pharma marketing texts.
- **What it covers:** Traditional pharma promotion, detailing, DTC, regulatory constraints, and brand planning.
- **What it misses:** AI model evaluation, programmatic HCP infrastructure, claims-driven attribution, EHR-triggered messaging, and current measurement gaps.
- **Why choose this book:** It treats the modern AI targeting stack as the central object rather than an add-on.

### Comparable Text 3

- **Title / Author / Year:** General machine learning and marketing analytics books.
- **What it covers:** Prediction, experimentation, segmentation, targeting, and marketing measurement.
- **What it misses:** Pharma-specific constraints: FDA label, MLR, fair balance, NPI identity, prescribing data, payer access, brand equity in prescription medicine, and patient-welfare misalignment.
- **Why choose this book:** It teaches model choice inside the real constraints of pharma commercial systems.

## Positioning Statements

- For Fellows: a playbook for turning current AI research into credible pharma marketing research projects.
- For product teams: a filter for deciding which external research ideas deserve engineering and commercial resources.
- For brand teams: a way to connect AI targeting to brand KPIs, not only campaign attribution.
- For instructors: a teachable bridge between healthcare marketing, AI methods, and applied innovation.

## Thesis Test

The proposed TOC reflects the thesis because it moves from system map to measurement stack, from model families to experiment design, and from research exploration to product decision. The sequence repeatedly asks: what can a Fellow test, what KPI would it improve, what evidence would justify investment, and what risk would make the idea unacceptable?
